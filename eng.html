AT THE DEAD OF NIGHT: A sonnet collection

Our sonnet collection "At the Dead of Night" aims to encapsulate the pervasive feeling of yearning and longing for one's missing half that transpire during hours of solitude and quietness.

"Otsukimi" by Frances de Leon

SHAKESPEAREAN, I Wanna Be Yours by Arctic Monkeys

Explosions occur every heartbeat
An undying spiral of desire stirs
Cracking walls, thumping on every drum beat
My Wings spread, clinging to the fondness that spurs
My being conforms to any shape or form you need
For as far as how you want your coffee brewed
How much dust I’m able to exceed

Chambers of the secrets to my heart go erratic

The weight of my love to the Pacific Ocean, unbreakable
Neither universe nor obstacle can halt my passion from going course
For in my heart, all it screams is “I wanna be yours”

PETRARCHAN, R U Mine? by Arctic Monkeys

Tied up by the idea of you, like a puppet hung by string.
Tracy island, time traveling, through all those crystal-lensed heartbreaks.
Oh, how time has come too late for our exchange on some daybreak.
A form of beauty it seems, out of reach yet a light that pulls on your heartstrings.
Every corner and space in my mind haywire with every absence recurring.
Mind, a stranger to this place, yearning manifests like outbreak
Contentment a shadow to your every whereabout, spoken pleas break
Control teeters the brink, in that deep mystery of yours is my presence worth endearing?
Reign over my very existence ceased for you are all I long, possibilities of diving headfirst now arise.
Creations of the worlds of you and I left unsaid, the very magic of our first stays
Unfortunate we’re forced to grow, chained by life’s endless chastise
My love for you transcends time and space, leaving me ablaze.
An angel in the dark, awakening my desires I must surmise.
Insanity envelops me, filled with the wish of yet again crossing ways.

PETRARCHAN, Arabella by Arctic Monkeys

Lain in coat wrought of cheetah, bewitchment sleeps.
I cannot help but seek for revelation in her mystique,
Yearning for a slip under the fabric, stylish and chic.
She pulls a cigarette from the box that she keeps,
And I dream to try the stick that rests against her lips as she speaks.
Oh, how I long to taste the smoke that trails past her cheek.
As her lips brush the rim of her drink, edge sleek,
Sitting under the dim lights, it’s like time sleeps.
When she tips a drink back, the whole world holds its breath,
Her fingers curl around the bottle, quick and deft.
I can’t help but wish to be the bottle, lying still in her hand.
I long for her fingers locked with mine, a promise made ‘till death,
Each sip stealing my soul, my heart ransacked in theft.
Her charm unsurpassed and her voice armed to enchant, like someone brought to earth to bring salvation to the damned.

SHAKESPEAREAN, Why'd You Only Call Me When You're High? by Arctic Monkeys

As all the clocks do not tick rearward
My lover’s bound departed incites dread
A halted dialogue you found absurd
“Am I only of appeal and of apt
Once the liquor has you trapped in its grasp?”
